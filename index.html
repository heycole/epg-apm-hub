🏠 Assistant Property Manager — Role Overview

As the Assistant Property Manager for EPG Management, you are responsible for daily operational execution across all key functional areas. You work directly with the Property Manager to ensure smooth operations, tenant satisfaction and property compliance.

Operations & Maintenance
  • Coordinate maintenance requests and vendor relationships
  • Track rental licenses and compliance requirements
  • Manage utility accounts: water, PECO and PGW
  • Oversee move-in and move-out procedures
  • Onboard new properties
  • Support general administrative and property management tasks as needed
Finance & Accounting
  • Accounts payable & receivable (bills, invoices, rent tracking)
  • Monitoring and tracking late rent
  • Identifying discrepancies and solving financial issues before they escalate
  • Trust accounting & monthly reconciliations
  • Managing rent money & escrow accounts
  • Preparing and processing monthly owner distributions
  • Supporting financial review processes and reporting
  • Assisting with company financial tracking
Leasing & Marketing
  • Manage leasing pipeline and rental applications
  • Process leads and have a weekly check-in with the Leasing Agent to see how showings are going
  • Post and update rental listings online and monitor their performance and marketing
Communications & Relationships
  • Handle tenant/owner communications and inquiries
  • Escalate issues appropriately to the Property Manager

📅 Monthly Calendar & Phases

The Assistant PM role follows monthly phases across all operational areas. Use this calendar to stay ahead of recurring activities.

Phase 1: Lease Management & Renewals (Days 1-7)

• Pull 90-day lease expiration report from AppFolio
• Send renewal notices to tenants with expiring leases
• Follow up on outstanding lease applications
• Process any approved leases and schedule move-ins

Phase 2: Listings & Marketing (Days 8-15)

• Review active listing performance from past month
• Refresh stale listings with new photos/description
• Pull metrics on lead sources and conversion rates
• Schedule photographer for any new vacant units

Phase 3: Maintenance & Compliance (Days 16-23)

• Audit all open work orders — follow up on stale ones
• Review rental license renewal deadlines
• Schedule annual/routine inspections
• Check utility accounts and verify tenant transfers

Phase 4: Reporting & Planning (Days 24-End)

• Compile monthly summary of all operational areas
• Review tenant communication history for any patterns
• Process month-end closings and final invoices
• Plan priorities for next month with Property Manager

🔧 Work Order Priority Guide
Emergency
Respond immediately. Gas leak, flooding/burst pipe, no heat in winter, fire damage, electrical hazard, security breach (broken locks/windows)
Urgent
Respond within 4 hours. No hot water, HVAC failure, plumbing backup/no water, broken exterior door/lock, refrigerator failure
Routine
Respond within 24-48 hours. Appliance repair, minor leak, cosmetic damage, pest control, light fixtures
Scheduled
Plan in advance. HVAC service, preventive inspections, turnover, landscaping, common area maintenance
📝 Creating a Work Order in AppFolio
  1. Open the work order — check if there are multiple items needing separate vendors
  2. If yes, create separate Service Requests for each vendor
  3. Click Create New Service Request in AppFolio
  4. Fill in all required fields:
    • Work Order Type: Internal → Resident
    • Entry Notice: Add 24-hour notice text if vendor needs entry
    • Priority: Emergency / Urgent / Routine / Scheduled
    • Description: Detailed issue description with photos
    • Vendor Instructions: Specific notes for the vendor
    • Vendor: Assign from approved vendor list
  5. Contact tenant for discovery — get photos, details, confirm access
  6. Determine if tenant-responsible (per lease) — if yes, communicate to tenant
👥 Vendor Dispatch & Scheduling

Three ways to schedule vendor work:

Option 1
Vendor dispatched through AppFolio Vendor Portal — they accept and schedule directly
Option 2
Message vendor in EPG Google Voice Group Chat to coordinate schedule
Option 3
Schedule on behalf of vendor — coordinate between vendor and tenant
⚠️ 24-Hour Entry Notice Required
Always provide tenant with 24 hours notice before vendor entry. Include notice text in the AppFolio work order.
📊 Follow-Up Requirement
Follow up on every open work order minimum 1x per day. No work order should go a full day without a status update.

📨 Submitting Proposal to Owner for Approval
  1. Create the work order in AppFolio with full details
  2. Get the vendor estimate / proposal document
  3. Attach the estimate to the work order
  4. Click "Request Owner for Approval" button in AppFolio
  5. Owner receives notification and can approve/deny in their portal
  6. Once approved, dispatch the vendor
  7. If denied, communicate back to tenant and close or modify the work order
💡 $250+ = Big Bill
Any job estimated at $250 or more is a "big bill." Get vendor pricing immediately and communicate to the owner before authorizing work.
✅ Job Completion & Invoice Process
  1. Confirm with vendor that the job is complete
  2. Confirm with tenant that the issue is resolved
  3. Ask who is at-fault (tenant or normal wear/tear) for billing purposes
  4. In AppFolio, go to ACTION → Create Bill → Billable Items
  5. GL Code: 6147 (Repairs-Other) for most maintenance work
  6. Verify the bill appears in Accounting → Payables
  7. Close the work order via ACTION → Mark Job Done
  8. DO NOT check "No Bill Needed" — always create the bill

🚫 Never Skip Billing
Every completed work order must have a bill created. "No Bill Needed" should never be checked.

🚫 Critical Blackout Zones
Bill Entry Blackout: No bills should be entered from the 8th of the month until the day after owner distributions are complete (except for rare exceptions).
Water Bill Blackout: Water bills cannot be paid from the 26th of the month until the day after owner distributions.
🏠 How to Add an Owner Bill

When billing an owner for a property expense, use the correct GL code. Common scenarios:

  1. Go to Accounting → Payables → Enter Bill
  2. Select the correct payee (vendor, city, etc.)
  3. Select the correct GL code:
    • 6200 for Licensing
    • 6173 for Water
    • 6147 for General Repairs
    • 6077 for Cleaning
    • 7010 for New Appliance Purchase
  4. Enter the amount and attach supporting documentation
  5. If unsure about the GL code, review with Nicole before posting
📄 How to Bill for Licensing
  1. Go to Accounting → Payables → Enter Bill
  2. Payee: City of Philadelphia
  3. GL Code: 6200 (Licensing)
  4. Enter the license fee amount
  5. Attach the receipt to the bill
  6. Submit for processing
🚫 Tenant Charge Deadline
All charges to a tenant's ledger must be posted no later than the 26th of the month. This ensures charges are reflected before the tenant's next rent payment processes and nothing gets missed on their statement.

🚰 Water Bill Process (3 Steps)

Water bills require three separate actions. All three must be completed.

Step 1: Pay the Water Bill
  1. Go to phila.gov and log in with EPG credentials
  2. Search the property address
  3. Pay the balance due
  4. Save the confirmation number
Step 2: Bill the Owner in AppFolio
  1. Go to Accounting → Payables → Enter Bill
  2. Payee: City of Philadelphia
  3. GL Code: 6173 (Water)
  4. Enter the amount paid
  5. Include confirmation number in description
  6. Attach the receipt/screenshot
Step 3: Bill the Tenant in AppFolio
  1. Search the property and find the tenant
  2. Click Enter Charge
  3. GL Code: 4320 (Tenant Water Bill — Income)
  4. Enter the same amount
  5. Note: This is an Income GL, not Expense
🔧 Vendor Invoice & Maintenance Billing

  4. Verify the bill appears in Accounting → Payables
  5. Do NOT check "No Bill Needed" — always create the bill

📚 SOPs & Documentation
⚖️ Compliance & Fair Housing

As the Assistant PM, you are responsible for ensuring all operations comply with fair housing laws, local regulations, and company policies. Any questions regarding compliance or fair housing should be escalated to the Property Manager immediately.

Fair Housing Requirements
  • Apply consistent screening criteria to all applicants
  • Never discriminate based on protected classes (race, color, religion, national origin, sex, familial status, disability)
  • Document all decisions with objective reasons
  • Reasonable accommodation requests go to Property Manager immediately
  • Service animal requests must be reviewed and documented
  • Never discuss fair housing violations with tenants or staff — escalate immediately
Lease & Rental Compliance
  • All leases must be reviewed by Property Manager before execution
  • Security deposit limits and handling per state law
  • Rent increase notice requirements (typically 30-60 days)
  • Move-out inspection and deposit return within state-required timeframe
  • Proper notice and cure periods for lease violations
  • Eviction procedures (when applicable, handled by Property Manager)
Property & Safety Compliance
  • Smoke detectors present and functional in all units and common areas
  • CO detectors present and functional (where required by local law)
  • Fire extinguishers in common areas
  • Habitable conditions: heat, water, electricity, roof, floors, windows
  • Lead paint disclosures for pre-1978 properties
  • Working locks on all entry doors and windows
  • Proper egress in bedrooms (operable windows or door)
